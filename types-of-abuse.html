Types of Abuse

Know the many forms abuse can take — they’re not always physical.

Physical Abuse

Any use of physical force that injures or endangers someone — hitting, slapping, pushing, or restraining.

Emotional Abuse

Manipulation, threats, humiliation, isolation, or controlling behavior intended to undermine confidence.

Verbal Abuse

Yelling, insults, name-calling, belittling, or constant criticism meant to hurt or dominate.

Sexual Abuse

Any unwanted sexual contact, coercion, or behavior used to assert power — even within relationships.

Financial Abuse

Controlling access to money, restricting work or education, or stealing income to create dependence.
